Questions

1. The RBI’s moratorium measures for customers after the lockdown must have put additional stress on BFL as this lead to asymmetry i.e. on one hand, BFL has to offer such moratoriums to their customers; while on the other, their market borrowings must be repaid on due dates. How did BFL control this situation? To what extent did TLTRO help?

Answer:

BFL has always had strong liquidity. BFL has learnt this from the previous crisis namely, the IL&FS crisis. TLTRO was not used by BFL. The effect of the moratorium was about 20-25%. Diversified portfolios have helped BFL to keep its liquidity high. Thus, the situation after the announcement of RBI’s moratoriums measures for customers was much under control for BFL as compared to other NBFCs.

(What is TLTRO?: RBI cannot directly give money to NBFCs firms, so RBI lends it to the banks and in turn forces banks to buy the bonds of these firms. NBFCs or Corporates can issue new bonds, and those bonds will be bought by banks using the TLTRO money. NBFCs can then use the money to pay back existing bond obligations.To ease liquidity pressure on NBFCs, the RBI has taken multiple actions including a Targeted Long-Term Repo Operation (TLTRO) for the sector of Rs. 50,000 crore and a special financing window through SIDBI, NABARD and National Housing Bank (NHB) of another Rs. 50,000 crore to enable financing NBFCs.)

(What is liquidity?: Liquidity refers to the ease with which an asset, or security, can be converted into ready cash without affecting its market price.)

2. Given the slowest GDP growth in the last 40 years, FY2020 credit costs were higher than FY2019. What steps did BFL take to manage its risk costs? (Estimated credit costs for FY21- Rs. 6000 crores to Rs. 6300 crores- Also include the lifetime loss estimate on account of COVID-19)

Answer:

The Company’s strategy of ‘acquire and cross-sell’ to manage cost and portfolio risk, based on the axiom that an existing customer poses significantly lower credit risk than a new customer, ensures lower risk across portfolios. The pandemic came with opportunities. Loans on digital products like laptops, phones increased.

BFL ended the year with a net NPA of 0.65% on a consolidated basis. The standalone net NPA stood at 0.79%. On a consolidated basis, the Company’s provision coverage on non-NPA assets, excluding the contingency provision, stood at 97 bps; and 159 bps including the contingency provision. BFL took several steps to manage its risk costs including lowering of AUM growth over four quarters of FY2020. It has also re-pivoted its mix towards lower risk assets like mortgages.

    • 14. How does zero-based budgeting work? How will its implementation help us to recover in the next quarter?

    Answer:

    Zero-based budgeting (ZBB) is a method of budgeting in which all expenses must be justified for each new period. The process of zero-based budgeting starts from a "zero base," and every function within an organization is analyzed for its needs and costs. Budgets are then built around what is needed for the upcoming period, regardless of whether each budget is higher or lower than the previous one. Rather than cutting down people we are using ZBB to manage our expenses.

    Because of its detail-oriented nature, zero-based budgeting may be a rolling process done over several years, with a few functional areas reviewed at a time by managers or group leaders. Zero-based budgeting can help lower costs by avoiding blanket increases or decreases to a prior period's budget. It is, however, a time-consuming process that takes much longer than traditional, cost-based budgeting.

    17. Although along with risk premium there are many factors that affect the interest rate calculation but risk premium rates usually vary by business, customer segment and geography. Is this the only reason why BFL’s interest rate is not constant for all customer segments?

    Answer:

    A risk premium is the investment return an asset is expected to yield in excess of the risk-free rate of return. An asset's risk premium is a form of compensation for investors. It represents payment to investors for tolerating the extra risk in a given investment over that of a risk-free asset.

    BFL is a data driven company. We categorise customers based on the risk associated with them. Higher the risk associated with a customer, higher is the interest rates. Lower the risk, lower is the interest rate.
